My Story

I discovered my passion for Science and Technology at a young age growing up in Santa Rosa, Ca. As a child, I always questioned how things worked and how they were designed. As a senior in high school, I took a Physics course that introduced me to the field of Engineering. During this time, I also began to wonder how my phone and technology worked. I was fascinated by the fact that I can touch a button, or screen and it would respond. I went on to community college, at Santa Rosa Junior College where I found a combination of my interests, and passion: Computer Science.

Community College, was also the time when I saw most of my friends drop out of school, and resort to a labor job in the fields. The closer I got to the transfer stage (from community college to university), the more of my friends traded school for field labor. Many young adults and teenagers that grow up seeing this the norm, limit themselves and their goals after high school.

I want to help younger generations from low income communities expand their opportunities and their dreams, by helping them find their passion in tech, education, and STEM, and unltimately giving them resources to pursue their goals. Today, I am looking for ways to expand my opportunities in my computer science and software engineering, as a developer and programmer, and I am also looking for ways to give to my community. I am a student at Univerversity of California, Santa Cruz where I will be graduating with my Computer Science degree in 2019.

I am an undergrad student constantly looking to get involved in activities that go beyond classroom learning. Here are the clubs and programs I am currently a part of.

Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE)

SHPE’s mission is to change lives by empowering the Hispanic community to realize its fullest potential and to impact the world through STEM awareness, access, support and development. Being part of SHPE gives me a community of students and professionals who are in STEM, and the opportunity to promote STEM to kids in the k-12, as well as college students.

Visit SHPE
MESA Engineering Achievement (MEP)

MEP supports educationally and economically disadvantaged students with resources to achieve success in school, career, and STEM-related disciplines. MEP provides me with advising, academic and career planning, and a place to study with other STEM students with a similar background.

Visit MEP
UC Santa Cruz Rotaract Club

UC Santa Cruz Rotaract is a group of students who volunteer their time to do community service. I have met some awesome people here, and the club is full of fun activities.
