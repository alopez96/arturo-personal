My Story

Early in my post secondary education, I studied at Santa Rosa Junior College, where I was a part of a year long program called Puente. Puente's mission is to increase the transfer rate of underrepresented students to universities. Puente consists of an English critical thinking course, and counseling course revolved around the transfer process, but the most valuable lesson I got from my Puente instructors, was about personal growth and community.

I remember we played a game early in the semester, and at the time, I considered it very silly, and I laughed at it. The professors took us outside on this sunny day to do an activity on the field next to the library. It was the place with most traffic on campus, with students going to the library, cafeteria, tutoring, book store, or simply hanging out. They set gray rubber reactangles on the grass, and the trail it followed led to the concrete walk way. The objective was to get across, but you didn't win until everybody made it across. It forced the person that found the best way to get there to turn around and help the those following. One after another, all helping each other.

Although this game did not teach me about the importance of community, it is something that I remember when I think about helping my peers succeed. Today, I am looking for ways to expand my opportunities in my computer science and software engineering, as a developer and programmer, and I am also looking for ways to give to my community and to help people who follow behind me. I am currently attending UC Santa Cruz, as a Computer Science student, and my aticipated date for my Bachelor's degree is Fall 2019.
